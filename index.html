About

I am a fourth year PhD candidate in Economics at Concordia University. My research develops econometric methods for credible inference with dependent data—advancing robust estimation and bootstrap inference for time-series moment-condition models under dependence and misspecification. I also work on extending nonlinear Difference-in-Differences and distributional policy evaluation methods to spatio-temporal settings.

You can find details on my work on the Research page.
